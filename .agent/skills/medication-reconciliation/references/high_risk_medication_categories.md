# High-Risk Medication Categories

Any discrepancy involving a medication from these categories must be classified as MEDIUM or HIGH risk.

## Anticoagulants
warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, acenocoumarol, heparin, enoxaparin

Interaction risk: NSAIDs, antiplatelet agents, some antibiotics.

## NSAIDs (Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs)
ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, ketoprofen, indomethacin, celecoxib, etoricoxib

Interaction risk: anticoagulants, antihypertensives, diuretics, corticosteroids.
Risk in elderly: GI bleeding, renal impairment, cardiovascular events.

## Diuretics
furosemide, torasemide, hydrochlorothiazide, spironolactone, chlorthalidone

Omission risk: fluid overload, heart failure decompensation.
Interaction risk: NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, potassium-sparing agents.

## Antidiabetics
insulin, metformin, glibenclamide, glipizide, sitagliptin, empagliflozin

Risk: hypoglycemia (especially sulfonylureas and insulin).
Omission risk: hyperglycemia, diabetic ketoacidosis.

## Antiplatelet agents
aspirin (antiplatelet doses), clopidogrel, ticagrelor, prasugrel

Interaction risk: anticoagulants, NSAIDs.

## Opioids
morphine, oxycodone, fentanyl, tramadol, codeine, buprenorphine

Risk: respiratory depression, constipation, falls in elderly.
Interaction risk: benzodiazepines, CNS depressants.

## Antiepileptics / Anticonvulsants
phenytoin, carbamazepine, valproate, levetiracetam, lamotrigine

Risk: seizures if omitted. Narrow therapeutic index for some (phenytoin, valproate).

## Benzodiazepines and Z-drugs
diazepam, lorazepam, alprazolam, clonazepam, zolpidem, zopiclone

Risk in elderly: falls, cognitive impairment, dependency.

## Antihypertensives (selected high-risk)
ACE inhibitors + NSAIDs combination, beta-blocker abrupt discontinuation.

## Narrow therapeutic index drugs
digoxin, lithium, theophylline, phenytoin, warfarin

Any dose or source discrepancy → HIGH risk.
